What Is PDRN and Why Is It Going Viral?

PDRN is a compound derived from salmon DNA that stimulates cell regeneration and collagen synthesis. Originally used in wound healing and medical aesthetics (injectable PDRN has been popular in Korean clinics for years), it's now being formulated into topical creams and serums that deliver visible anti-aging results at a fraction of the cost of clinical treatments.

The TikTok format driving this trend is the before/after transformation video. Creators document 2-4 weeks of daily PDRN cream use, showing progressive improvement in fine lines, skin texture, and overall radiance. The format works because PDRN produces visibly noticeable results faster than retinol — users report seeing improvements in skin healing and smoothness within 7-10 days, compared to the 8-12 week wait for retinol.

This quick-results factor is what's causing the growth rate to spike. Retinol is effective but notoriously slow, with a 4-6 week "purging" phase where skin gets worse before it gets better. PDRN offers a gentler, faster alternative with the same collagen-boosting endpoint — and TikTok's skincare community has latched onto that comparison hard.

Beauty content consistently generates the highest engagement rates of any ecommerce category on TikTok, and skincare transformation videos are at the top of that heap. The combination of visible results, a compelling "new ingredient" narrative, and the Korean skincare halo (which carries massive credibility in beauty circles) creates a product that practically markets itself.

This is the highest-LTV demographic in beauty ecommerce — skincare enthusiasts don't just buy once, they build routines.
